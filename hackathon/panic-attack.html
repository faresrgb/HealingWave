How to Handle a Panic Attack

What to Do Instantly During a Panic Attack

A panic attack can be overwhelming and scary, but remember: it will pass. Here are some steps to help you regain control:

  • Breathe Deeply: Inhale slowly through your nose for a count of 4, hold your breath for 4 seconds, and exhale slowly through your mouth for 6 seconds. Repeat this several times.
  • Ground Yourself: Use the 5-4-3-2-1 technique:
    • Identify 5 things you can see.
    • Identify 4 things you can touch.
    • Identify 3 things you can hear.
    • Identify 2 things you can smell.
    • Identify 1 thing you can taste.
  • Repeat a Mantra: Say to yourself, "This will pass. I am safe. I can handle this."
  • Relax Your Muscles: Try progressive muscle relaxation by tensing each muscle group for 5 seconds and then relaxing them.
  • Focus on an Object: Choose an object in your environment and describe it in detail to yourself. This helps divert your mind from the panic.
Quick Tip: Keep a grounding object, such as a smooth stone or a fidget tool, with you to use in moments of distress.

If panic attacks persist, consider reaching out to a healthcare professional or a mental health counselor for further support.
